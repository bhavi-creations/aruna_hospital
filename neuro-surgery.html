Laminectomy

Laminectomy: Relieving Spinal Pressure

Laminectomy is a surgical procedure that aims to alleviate pressure on the spinal cord or nerves in the spine. During a laminectomy, a portion of the vertebral bone called the lamina is removed, creating space and reducing compression on the spinal cord or nerves. This procedure is often performed to treat conditions such as spinal stenosis or herniated discs, which can cause pain, weakness, or numbness in the back and legs. Laminectomy can provide relief and improve mobility for individuals experiencing these symptoms.

Consider a laminectomy if you're seeking relief from spinal pressure and related symptoms.
